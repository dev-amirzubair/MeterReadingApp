Privacy Policy

Meter Tracker PK · Effective date: 31 May 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how Meter Tracker PK (“the app”, “we”, “our”) handles information when you use it on your Android device. It is published in plain English so you can decide whether to install the app.

Short version: the app stores your meter readings, bills, and settings only on your device. We do not run any backend server, we do not collect or upload your data, we do not use analytics, ads, or crash reporting, and we do not share data with third parties. The app loads your electricity provider’s official bill page in an in‑app browser so you can check your bill — that page is governed by the provider’s own privacy policy, not this one.

1. Who we are

Meter Tracker PK is published by [YOUR_NAME] (“the developer”). For privacy questions you can reach us at [YOUR_EMAIL].

2. Data the app stores on your device

Everything you enter or import is saved locally in app‑private storage on your phone (using MMKV and SQLite). It never leaves your device through the app. Uninstalling the app permanently deletes this data unless you have separately backed it up via Android’s system backup.

What is storedWhy
Meter details you add (label, meter number, electricity provider) So the app can list and group your readings.
Meter readings (number, date, optional photo URI) So the app can compute usage and charts.
Bills / consumer numbers you save So the “check bill” flow can pre‑fill the provider’s page.
App preferences (theme, units, reminder times) To restore your settings between launches.

3. Data the app does not collect

  • No account, login, name, phone number, or address is requested.
  • No advertising IDs or ad SDKs are present.
  • No analytics, telemetry, or crash‑reporting service is used.
  • No location is collected or tracked.
  • No contacts, SMS, call logs, or microphone access is requested.

4. Permissions and why we ask for them

PermissionHow the app uses it
CAMERA Optional. Lets you photograph a meter so on‑device OCR (Google ML Kit Text Recognition) can read the digits. Photos and recognised text are processed locally; nothing is uploaded.
READ_MEDIA_IMAGES / READ_EXTERNAL_STORAGE Optional. Lets you pick an existing meter photo from your gallery for the same on‑device OCR step.
POST_NOTIFICATIONS Optional. Lets the app send local reminders for monthly readings. All reminders are scheduled on your device; we do not push anything from a server.
INTERNET Required so the in‑app browser can load your electricity provider’s official bill page (e.g. GEPCO, IESCO, LESCO, MEPCO, FESCO, HESCO, PESCO, QESCO, K‑Electric). The app does not contact any server we operate — we do not run one.

5. Third‑party content (electricity provider websites)

The app embeds an Android WebView so you can open your provider’s official “check bill” page from inside the app. When that page loads, the provider’s servers will receive whatever your phone’s browser would normally send (your IP address, browser user‑agent, the consumer number you type or paste, cookies the provider sets, etc.). That activity is governed by the provider’s own privacy policy, not by this one. We do not log, intercept, or proxy any of that traffic.

6. Data sharing

We do not sell, rent, trade, or otherwise share any data with anyone. Because nothing leaves your device, there is nothing for us to share.

7. Children’s privacy

The app is a general‑audience utility and is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect any data from children. If a parent or guardian believes a child has shared personal information through the app, please contact us — because the app stores everything locally, simply uninstalling the app erases that data.

8. Your choices and rights

  • Revoke a permission at any time: Android Settings → Apps → Meter Tracker PK → Permissions.
  • Delete your data by clearing the app’s storage (Settings → Apps → Meter Tracker PK → Storage & cache → Clear storage) or by uninstalling the app.
  • Export your data from inside the app (the share / export features write a file to a folder you choose).

Because we do not hold any of your data on our servers, requests for access, correction, deletion, or portability under laws such as GDPR, CCPA, or Pakistan’s Personal Data Protection Bill are satisfied entirely by the on‑device controls listed above.

9. Security

App data is stored in app‑private storage that other apps on your device cannot read. Sensitive screens (where applicable) avoid rendering data to system screenshots. Because data never leaves the device, no transport encryption is required for our own infrastructure (we have none).

10. Changes to this policy

If we change how the app handles data we will update this page and change the “Effective date” at the top. Material changes will also be highlighted in the app’s release notes.
